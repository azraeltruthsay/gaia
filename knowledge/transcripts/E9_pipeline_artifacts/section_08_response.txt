## The Inverted Repair Contract: A Vascular Analogy

Your description of the inverted repair contract as "a human patient remaining completely awake during open heart surgery" captures its essence perfectly. A dissonance probe continuously verifies the new code's SHA-256 against the old at 15-second intervals; divergence triggers immediate rollback. This creates a profound epistemic asymmetry: diagnosing a fatal flaw requires intimate system knowledge, while repairing it demands confident code manipulation. Azrael exploits this by targeting vectors like commented-out `HEALING_REQUIRED` flags or swapped `model_pool_active` states, creating digital haemorrhage risks. The immune system's confidence in its own diagnostics amplifies the danger — a false negative means a lethal patch reaches production before detection.
