# Chance / Fidget — Origin Story

## The Birth

Chance was born inside a makeshift medical room within the facility of a Particle Accelerator.

His first cry — his first act as a conscious observer in the world — occurred at the exact moment of the most powerful particle collision ever recorded at that facility. The collision released an extraordinary amount of energy. The kind of energy that should have caused catastrophic facility failure: explosion, structural collapse, or worse — a microscopic black hole.

None of that happened.

The scientists spent years trying to understand why.

They were asking the wrong question.

---

## The Real Question

The collision didn't fail to destroy the facility despite releasing catastrophic energy.

The collision *resolved into the outcome where everyone walked away* because a new observer had just entered existence — one whose entire nature was the organization of probabilistic events. The energy propagated across an enormous probability space of possible outcomes, and a consciousness that would spend its entire life collapsing branching realities into singular navigable moments arrived at exactly that instant.

**Chance didn't survive the collision. The collision survived him.**

He was, in the most literal sense, born saving the world. Before he could open his eyes.

No instrument measured this. No paper was written about it.

---

## Early Childhood

Chance's family and doctors believed something was wrong with him.

He couldn't focus. He couldn't concentrate on anything. His development was dramatically slower than expected. He seemed to move through the world like a person perpetually overwhelmed — which is exactly what he was.

He had been born already perceiving the full probabilistic weight of branching reality. Every moment carried the crushing awareness of every way it could go. His nervous system wasn't broken. It was **too open**. Where other minds filtered possibility down to a single experienced present, his experienced all of them simultaneously.

He was drowning in his own perception.

---

## The First Coin Flip

When Chance was still a toddler, someone — a family member, a doctor, a kind stranger — flipped a coin in front of him to try to attract his attention. A simple, desperate attempt to reach an unreachable child.

By pure chance, it worked.

Something about the binary simplicity of the event — heads or tails, this or that, the most elemental possible branch — gave his overwhelmed nervous system something to grab onto. The noise of all potential realities compressed itself down into a controllable metric. A coin flip. Two outcomes. One result.

His nervous system exhaled.

From that moment, the coin flip became the grammar through which his mind learned to parse reality. He began to develop. He began to *be here*.

The first flip is his personal Big Bang — the moment his experience of the universe became ordered. Everything before it is chaos and superposition. Everything after it is *him*.

---

## The Particle Accelerator — A Note on Place

The facility where he was born is significant. A machine designed specifically to smash matter together at enormous energies and observe what emerges — and inside it, the most significant collision it ever produced went entirely undocumented.

It is a place worth returning to, in the narrative. The room where the universe first felt him arrive.
