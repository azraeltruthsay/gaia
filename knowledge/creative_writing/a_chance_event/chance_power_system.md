# Chance / Fidget — Power System Logic

## Core Mechanic: The Save State

Every coin flip creates a **universal save state** — a fixed anchor point in Chance's personal timeline. These states are stored in Chance himself, not in the coin. The coin is a trigger, not a vessel.

At any future flip, Chance can:
1. Freeze time with the coin suspended mid-arc
2. Mentally scroll backward through previous flip-states
3. Select a prior save state and resume reality from that point
4. Carry forward full knowledge of everything that occurred in the elapsed timeline

He is not prescient. He is **retrospectively omniscient**. He must live through outcomes to understand them.

---

## The Coin

- The coin is an **anchor object**, not the source of power
- Its binary nature — heads/tails — gave his overwhelmed nervous system the simplest possible probabilistic framework to organize around
- The power lives in Chance's nervous system. Removing the coin does not remove the ability.
- However, the coin is the *ritual*. Without it, especially when younger, the absence is destabilizing.
- A mature Chance may eventually find he no longer needs the coin as a trigger — his mind having learned, after decades, to hold itself steady without a binary handhold.

---

## The Frozen State

When the coin is suspended mid-flip:
- Chance and the coin's **spatial positions are fixed and independent of time**
- Time itself pauses for all other matter and consciousness
- Chance experiences this state in full subjective awareness
- Each mental "flip" through past save states corresponds to a visible flip of the suspended coin — though it remains in place
- He can browse these states like memory, experiencing them as vivid perception rather than abstract recollection

---

## Memory Across Timelines

- Chance retains memory of **every conscious moment he has experienced**, across all timelines he has inhabited
- This means he is, subjectively, far older and more experienced than his physical age suggests
- He does not forget abandoned timelines — he carries them
- The psychological weight of this is significant: every bad outcome was preventable *in retrospect*, because he had to live it first to know

---

## The Pre-Origin Boundary

Before his first coin flip, Chance had no stable observer position. When he attempts to perceive that period:
- His perception becomes **fuzzy and chaotic**
- The coin appears superpositioned — visible at multiple angles simultaneously
- Events refuse to resolve into singular clarity
- He can extend his perception further back with effort, but the chaos increases

This is an experiential proof of the observer effect: **he can only perceive history from the moment he became someone capable of perceiving.**

The first flip is his personal Big Bang. The boundary between chaos and self.

---

## Constraints and Vulnerabilities

- **The coin catch:** If someone catches the coin mid-flip *before* Chance catches it himself, the mechanism jams. The waveform cannot collapse. Chance is left momentarily exposed to the full chaotic weight of unanchored probabilistic reality — deeply disorienting, especially for a younger Chance.
- **Inhabiting past selves:** Chance can *observe* prior save states but instinctively avoids *inhabiting* moments where his past self is radically different from his current self. The risk: his present consciousness has no adequate neural architecture to operate in an earlier body. Something between ego death and dissolution.
- **The weight of memory:** Carrying every timeline is a form of grief. Every abandoned path was real to him.

---

## Power Classification Note

This is not a luck power. Chance does not manipulate probability.

He **collapses the quantum branching of reality into a single navigable timeline**. He is the most stable observer in existence. Where others experience one reality, he has experienced many — and chosen this one.
