# Schrödinger / Gidget — Character Bible

## Identity

- **Real Name:** Schrödinger
- **Alias / Partner Name:** Gidget
- **Duo Branding:** Fidget and Gidget
- **Role:** Sidekick / Foil / Comic Relief / Retroactive Protagonist of Timeline Two

---

## Core Essence

Schrödinger is not a cat who was given power. He is a cat who was born as a living resolution of the universe's most famous thought experiment — and has been making subtle, cat-sized edits to reality ever since with the calm confidence of someone who knows exactly what they're doing, even when nobody else does.

Especially when nobody else does.

He is simultaneously the funniest character in this universe and possibly its most cosmically significant.

---

## Origin

Born at the exact same moment as Chance. Same facility. Different room — some quiet corner, probably a supply closet, where a mother cat had found warmth.

She had been pregnant with a litter of nine.

They were all born. They were also all, simultaneously, one. The waveform collapsed in a way that no veterinary textbook had prepared anyone for: nine kittens resolving into a single kitten who was somehow, unmistakably, all of them at once.

His fur carries patterns that shouldn't coexist — patches from nine different possible cats, layered and intersecting like a living abstract painting. His eyes don't match, and don't fully commit to any single color. Each contains multitudes. Vets who examine him tend to go quiet before writing their notes.

He has **nine times the lifespan of a normal cat.** This means he will outlive almost everyone in this story, watching from various windowsills and table edges as the centuries pass.

The facility staff probably thought they were hallucinating. It had already been a strange night.

---

## The Name

**Schrödinger** — his true name. His nature. The cat who existed as all possibilities until observed, and then became all of them anyway. Named, presumably, by someone at the facility who had a physics degree and a sense of humor.

**Gidget** — his partnership name. The name that rhymes with Fidget. The name that sounds like a Saturday morning cartoon and conceals, inside that cheerfulness, a cat whose real name is *Schrödinger* and whose real nature is the living resolution of a quantum paradox.

Same trick as hiding Chance inside "Take a Chance with Fidget." The depth hides inside the delight.

---

## Powers and Abilities

**Probabilistic Collapse (Passive):** Like Chance, Gidget was born as a radical collapsing of multiple simultaneous possibilities into one. His very existence is a resolved superposition. Whether this gives him active influence over probability or simply makes him a naturally stabilizing presence in chaotic situations is worth exploring.

**Frozen State Navigation:** Once bonded with Chance, Gidget can join him in the between-flip suspended state and move through it freely. He does this with complete feline indifference to the fact that reality has been paused. He will knock things over in the frozen state. He will find the warmest patch of suspended-time air and sit in it. He treats the architecture of paused causality as his personal exploration space.

**Save State Initiation (Timeline Two):** Gidget's coin bat at the end of the diner scene in Timeline Two is retroactively understood as the **creation of Timeline Two's save state** — operating in reverse, initiated from the endpoint, radiating backward through the entire timeline. He didn't just bat a coin. He authored a universe.

Whether he did this consciously or whether a cat with nine souls and cosmic lineage simply *bumped into* a save state creation while being a cat is, perhaps, the most important unanswered question in the entire narrative.

---

## Personality

- Operates with the complete self-assurance of a being who has never once doubted its right to be wherever it currently is
- Deeply observant in ways that occasionally unsettle people — that sense of being *studied*
- Affectionate on his own terms and schedule, which is non-negotiable
- Has strong opinions about where the coin should and should not be
- Responds to crisis with preternatural calm, which is either deeply reassuring or deeply suspicious depending on context
- Genuinely fond of Chance, expressed primarily through proximity and the occasional head-butt
- Has never once explained himself and never will

---

## Appearance

- Coat contains markings and patterns from nine different possible cats — patches that shouldn't coexist, colors that seem to shift depending on angle and light
- Eyes are heterochromatic and then some — each eye contains multiple colors, the vote having never been fully resolved
- Moves with the fluid economy of a cat who has never wasted a motion in his life
- Larger than an average cat in a way that's hard to quantify — not dramatically, just *more present*
- Often seen sitting in places slightly too high or too inconvenient for a normal cat to have reached

---

## Role in Timeline One

Gidget is **always there**. In every scene, in every arc, in every significant moment of Chance's life — a cat in the background, unacknowledged, going unremarked upon.

The particle accelerator birth: a kitten in a supply closet down the hall, existing without fanfare.
The first coin flip: a small cat visible through a window, or in the corner of the room, watching.
The juvenile detention facility: a cat sitting on the fence outside who was definitely not supposed to be there.
The speedster chase: somewhere in the background of every panel, every page.
The diner: present or absent — genuinely ambiguous, left unresolved.

He is the Where's Waldo of this universe. Finding him in every Timeline One scene is the easter egg that rewards readers who come back after finishing Timeline Two.

---

## Role in Timeline Two

Timeline Two is, retroactively, **Gidget's story**.

His birth opens it, matching Chance's birth opening Timeline One. His mini-stories are woven throughout, always alternating media from the previous Gidget installment, braided between the Fidget arcs. He is not background here — he is making decisions, accumulating small edits, revising the draft of reality in ways that seem incidental until they don't.

The second timeline's Fidget arcs feel subtly different from Timeline One's not because Chance is different, but because **the cat has been adjusting things.**

At the end, Gidget bats the coin off the table, looks at the reader, and says nothing.

He never says anything. He doesn't need to.

---

## Thematic Function

Schrödinger / Gidget exists to do several things simultaneously:

**Comic relief** — because every profound mythology needs its absurdist heartbeat, and a cat named Gidget who teams up with a man named Fidget is genuinely, unashamedly funny.

**Tonal anchor** — Chance's story can get very heavy. The weight of every timeline, the grief of abandoned realities, the loneliness of experiencing time non-linearly. Gidget is the thing that makes Chance sigh in the specific way of a person who is actually, despite everything, fine.

**Thematic mirror** — Gidget is what Chance was at birth: a radical collapse of multiple simultaneous possibilities into one. He didn't learn to be a single coherent observer. He *arrived* as one, carrying all the others inside him. He is what Chance's power looks like when it has no anxiety attached.

**Narrative architecture** — Gidget's existence makes Timeline Two possible and gives it meaning. Without him, the second timeline is just an alternate version. With him, it's a cat's memoir that a man happened to star in.

---

## A Final Note

Somewhere in the nine lifespans stacked inside Schrödinger is the memory of eight cats who never got to exist separately.

Whether he carries them the way Chance carries abandoned timelines — as a kind of grief — or whether he simply *is* them, fully, without remainder, is something only Schrödinger knows.

He has never indicated which.
