# Chance / Fidget — The Speedster Arc

## Context

During his teenage years, Chance was a menace. Not malicious by nature, but chaotic — using a cosmic power with the impulsivity and selfishness of adolescence. Rewriting outcomes for convenience. Avoiding consequences. Optimizing for the short game without regard for the world around him.

The public didn't know his name. They knew his habit. They called him **Fidget**.

---

## The Chase

A speedster was dispatched to stop him.

On paper this should have been straightforward. In practice it was anything but.

Speed is irrelevant against an opponent who keeps changing which reality you're chasing them through. The speedster would arrive where Fidget was — and Fidget had already flipped, folded time, and reappeared somewhere else. Not because he was faster. Because he was retroactively *relocating the chase itself*. The speedster wasn't being outrun. He was being made to chase the wrong timeline.

This went on for longer than anyone would like to admit.

---

## The Solution

The speedster had to stop thinking like a speedster.

Stop chasing the boy. **Chase the coin.**

The flip initiates the save state, but the *catch* — Fidget catching the coin himself — is what collapses the waveform and locks in the new reality. That moment, the brief arc of the coin before it returns to his hand, is the only window of vulnerability in the entire mechanism.

A speedster moving at full velocity could intercept that arc.

The first time it worked, Fidget was left standing there with empty hands, staring at the coin in someone else's grip — suddenly exposed to the full unanchored weight of probabilistic reality pressing in without a threshold to hold it back. For a teenager whose nervous system had spent its entire life depending on that handhold, it wasn't just a tactical defeat.

It was terrifying.

The speedster, moving too fast to fully register the boy's expression, probably didn't understand why it worked as well as it did. But it worked.

---

## Detention

Fidget was placed in a juvenile detention facility.

His coin was confiscated.

For a period — the exact duration worth developing in narrative — he had to live in a single timeline like everyone else. No save states. No rollbacks. Consequences arrived and stayed. Outcomes were permanent.

This is the real crucible of his character. Not the particle accelerator. Not the first coin flip. **Detention.** Being forced to experience reality as an ordinary person and discovering, slowly, painfully, that he could survive it.

He came out different. Not fixed — there is nothing to fix — but **oriented toward something**.

---

## The Partnership

When Fidget was released and began his path toward becoming a hero, the speedster who caught him became his closest partner.

The dynamic is specific and irreplaceable:
- The speedster is one of the only beings alive who has genuinely surprised Chance — who found the seam in his power and slipped through it
- Chance is one of the only beings alive who made the speedster feel, however briefly, like raw speed wasn't enough
- They understand each other's vulnerabilities with an intimacy that no one else shares
- The speedster knows where Chance is exposed. Chance knows the speedster can think fast enough to see past the obvious move.

They are each other's best check. And most trusted partner.

---

## The Name Goes Public

When Fidget became a hero, a decision was made about identity.

The slogan landed: **"Take a Chance with Fidget."**

His real name — Chance — was hidden inside it, in plain sight, from the moment of his public debut. Most people never noticed. The ones who did felt like they'd found something.

He was always Fidget to the world. He was always Chance underneath.
