## 📽️ Visual Imagery
A man sits on a small balcony atop a tall apartment building at dusk. Misty rain is falling. He flips a coin. At its zenith, the coin stops.

## 🕹️ The "Jog" Mechanic
- **Micro-Adjustments:** Chance "flicks" the air near the suspended coin. The rain stutters, reverses, and moves up. He isn't learning; he's "warming up," like a jogger doing a hop-walk at the start of a path.
- **The Hard Flick:** He flicks the coin with force. Reality becomes a blur of chaos—a "Probabilistic Smear."
- **Navigation:** He watches the path of the coin through time and space, deciding where to "exit" the smear.
- **The Merge:** When he chooses an event, he sees his past self. To interact, he simply moves to exactly where his current self is, and the two versions of his consciousness merge.

## 🧠 The Neural Constraint (The "Fixed" Past)
- **Neural Architecture:** Chance avoids inhabiting a past self that is radically younger than his current self.
- **The Risk:** His present consciousness lacks the compatible "neural hardware" to operate in a child's brain. It leads to ego dissolution or "mismatch failure."
- **The Result:** His timeline becomes "fixed" as he ages. He can view his early years as an observer, but he can no longer rewrite them. He has outgrown his own history.

## ✍️ Narrative Potential
This scene serves as a "Hub Event." The flashes he sees in the "smear" are **Nested Mini-Events**—perfect candidates for the visual medium to explore without requiring full prose chapters.
