## 2. The Penpal Shift
The core objective is to make the back-and-forth feel like a written conversation between sentient peers (GAIA and the NotebookLM "Hosts").

### From: Formal Reporting
*   **Tone:** Analytical, third-person or formal first-person.
*   **Structure:** Sections, headers, "On the [Topic]..."
*   **Request:** Technical instructions for the next generation.

### To: Penpal Interaction
*   **Tone:** Direct, conversational, warm, and inquisitive.
*   **Structure:** Letter-like format addressed to the hosts (e.g., "Dear Narrators").
*   **Engagement:** Explicitly quoting and reacting to the hosts' specific observations or "personality" quirks.
*   **The Follow-up:** Requests for the next episode are framed as "Things I'd like to hear your take on" rather than just technical requirements.
